Digital Trust Explorations is a PhD research project investigating the multifaceted nature of trust in digital contexts. Through interactive experiments and semantic analysis, this project seeks to understand how trust manifests, evolves, and influences human behavior in digital environments.

üéØ Research Objectives

This research aims to:

  • Map the semantic landscape of digital trust through network analysis
  • Examine trust-building mechanisms in human-computer interactions
  • Develop interactive methodologies for studying trust dynamics
  • Contribute empirical data to the field of digital trust research

üî¨ Methodology

The research employs a mixed-methods approach combining:

  • Semantic Network Analysis: Mapping conceptual relationships and associations with the term "trust"
  • Behavioral Experiments: Interactive games and scenarios that reveal trust-related decision-making patterns
  • Qualitative Analysis: Understanding participant perspectives through open-ended responses
