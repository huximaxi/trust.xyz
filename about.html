Digital Trust Explorations is a PhD research project investigating the multifaceted nature of trust in digital contexts. Through interactive experiments and semantic analysis, this project seeks to understand how trust manifests, evolves, and influences human behavior in digital environments.

üéØ Research Objectives

This research aims to:

  • Examine trust-building mechanisms in human-computer interactions
  • Develop interactive methodologies for studying trust dynamics
  • Contribute empirical data to the field of digital trust research

üî¨ Methodology

The Research employs a mixed-methods approach combining:

  • Behavioral Experiments: Interactive games and scenarios that reveal trust-related decision-making patterns
  • Qualitative Analysis: Understanding participant perspectives through open-ended responses

üåê Interactive Research Tools

The main idea behind this website is to host an expanding number of interactive trust experiments designed to collect data while providing participants with engaging experiences:

  • Semantic Network Map: An interactive visualization exploring semantic connections
  • Game Theology: A behavioral experiment examining trust-based decision making
  • More experiments coming soon...

Research findings and publications will be shared here as the project progresses.

ü§ù Participation

This research relies on voluntary participation. All experiments are designed with ethical considerations in mind, and participant data is handled according to research ethics guidelines. By participating in any experiment, you contribute to advancing our understanding of digital trust.

Privacy

We don't use cookies, don't store personal data of any kind. Trust experiments on the site only store the input you gave in the questionnaire, which is completely anon and not identifiable with any natural person. Always use a VPN for your connection though, the internet is a wild place!
