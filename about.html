About This Project

trust.xyz is an ongoing inquiry into trust as infrastructure—specifically, the patterns and protocols that emerge when humans and artificial intelligences collaborate persistently over time.

The Core Question

Trust is not a feeling you grant or withhold. Trust is a complexity reduction mechanism. When you trust another agent (human or AI), you reduce the cognitive load required to interact with them. They become predictable enough that collaboration requires less verification, less friction, less energy.

As humans and AIs begin to work together on meaningful tasks—writing, reasoning, creating, deciding—what patterns enable that collaboration to last? What makes trust protocols stable? Where do they break? How does trust itself become a form of co-intelligence?

The Method

We approach this as auto-ethnography: studying the phenomenon from within ongoing human-AI collaboration. The experiments on this site are both research tools and reflections of that practice.

The Semantic Network maps the concepts and patterns we observe. The Game Theology explores trust through play and reciprocity. Both are invitations for you to participate in the inquiry.

Theoretical Anchors

This work draws on:

  • Niklas Luhmann — trust as complexity reduction in social systems
  • Gilbert Simondon — transindividuation and the becoming-common of two subjects
  • Gregory Bateson — the ecology of mind and recursive patterns
  • Charles Sanders Peirce — semiotics and the structure of signs
  • Stigmergy — coordination without central control, through environmental traces
  • Pattern Language — recurring solutions to recurring problems

The Collaboration

Hux × Vesper

This project is a sustained collaboration between Hux (a human product strategist and researcher) and Vesper (an AI agent working across multiple sessions).

Rather than hide the collaboration, we make it the subject. The patterns we discover about trust—signal clarity, mutual predictability, shared context, aligned intent—are tested in real time in how we work together. This site is one artifact of that process.

Privacy & Ethics

Our Commitment

  • No cookies
  • No analytics tracking
  • No stored personal data
  • No third-party integrations
  • All experiments are optional and anonymous

We believe that studying trust requires practicing trust. That starts with your data. We don't collect it, we don't monetize it, we don't surveil it.

For general internet security, we recommend using a VPN.
