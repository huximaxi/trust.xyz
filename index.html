📚 Research

We approach trust as complexity reduction in co-intelligence systems. Drawing on Luhmann's trust theory, Simondon's transindividuation, Bateson's ecology of mind, and stigmergy as a coordination principle, we develop a framework for understanding how persistent human-AI collaboration stabilizes around shared protocols and mutual predictability.

This isn't about AI "earning" trust or humans "granting" it. Trust emerges when both parties reduce the cognitive load of interaction—when the other becomes sufficiently predictable that collaboration requires less verification, less friction. Through auto-ethnographic research and interactive experiments, we trace the patterns of trust infrastructure: how signals work, where breakdowns happen, what makes certain protocols resilient.
